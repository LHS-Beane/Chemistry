Chemistry Project

Stoichiometry in the Real World

You have been grinding through moles and stoichiometry problems. Now it is time to show where this chemistry actually matters. Your job is to investigate a real reaction, process, product, or event and explain the chemistry behind it.

Your Mission

Choose one chemistry topic connected to moles and stoichiometry. Research how the reaction works, why it matters, and how chemists use mole ratios to predict or control what happens.

Work Style

This is an individual project. You are responsible for your own research, explanation, and presentation.

Time Frame

You will have 2 class days to research, build, and prepare to present.

Final Goal

Teach the class how your topic connects to balanced equations, mole ratios, and real-life chemistry.

Interesting Topic Choices

Pick a topic that sounds dramatic, useful, surprising, or just plain interesting. Hover over a card to learn more.

Safety

Airbags

A tiny packet causes a life-saving event in milliseconds.

Airbags

Explosive decomposition reactions rapidly produce gas to inflate airbags. Great for connecting mass of reactant to moles and gas produced.

Entertainment

Fireworks

Colors, sparks, and explosions all come from chemistry.

Fireworks

Combustion and metal salts create bright colors and bursts. Good for linking chemical compounds, products, and controlled reaction amounts.

Engineering

Rocket Fuel

The right chemical ratio can launch a machine into space.

Rocket Fuel

Fuel and oxidizer ratios determine thrust and efficiency. A strong topic for mole ratios and limiting reactants in real engineering.

Health

Antacids

A small tablet can neutralize stomach acid.

Antacids

Neutralization reactions reduce acid in the stomach. Great for showing how many moles of acid one dose can handle.

Biology

Photosynthesis

Plants build glucose from water and carbon dioxide.

Photosynthesis

Plants convert CO₂ and water into glucose using light energy. This topic links stoichiometry to life on Earth and energy storage.

Technology

Hydrogen Fuel Cells

A chemical reaction can make electricity with water as a product.

Hydrogen Fuel Cells

Hydrogen reacts with oxygen to produce electricity and water. A modern topic that fits cleanly with balanced equations and mass relationships.

Materials

Rusting

A slow reaction can destroy metal over time.

Rusting

Oxidation reactions slowly corrode metals. Strong for connecting reactants in the air to mass gain and material damage.

Food

Baking Chemistry

Gas-producing reactions make baked goods rise.

Baking Chemistry

Baking soda and baking powder create carbon dioxide gas. This topic is familiar, practical, and easy to turn into a clear stoichiometry example.

Industry

Haber Process

Industrial chemistry helps feed the world.

Haber Process

Ammonia production from nitrogen and hydrogen supports fertilizer production worldwide. Great for showing chemistry at massive scale.

Everyday Use

Hand Warmers

A packet in your glove can release noticeable heat.

Hand Warmers

Exothermic reactions such as iron oxidation release heat. A nice choice for students who want a simple but real application.

Everyday Use

Chemical Ice Packs

Some reactions absorb heat and get cold fast.

Chemical Ice Packs

Endothermic processes absorb energy from the surroundings. This gives students a chance to connect chemistry to sports medicine and first aid.

Earth Science

Calcium Carbonate

This common compound shows up in shells, chalk, and limestone.

Calcium Carbonate

It reacts with acids and appears in natural materials all around us. A good bridge between chemistry, geology, and everyday materials.

Environment

Acid Rain

Pollution can turn into damaging acids in the atmosphere.

Acid Rain

Atmospheric reactions form sulfuric and nitric acids that damage ecosystems and buildings. Strong environmental relevance.

Transportation

Combustion Engines

Cars depend on the right fuel and oxygen balance.

Combustion Engines

Fuel-air ratios influence how efficiently engines work. A familiar application that ties directly to balanced combustion equations.

Lab Chemistry

Electrolysis

Electricity can break compounds apart.

Electrolysis

Electric current splits compounds like water into smaller substances. This blends chemistry, energy, and real industrial uses.

Medicine / Lab

Hydrogen Peroxide

A familiar liquid can break down and release oxygen gas.

Hydrogen Peroxide

Decomposition reactions release oxygen and are used in cleaning and lab demonstrations. Very approachable for students who want a clear equation.

Want a different topic?

You may propose your own topic if it clearly connects to a balanced chemical equation, moles, and stoichiometry.

What Your Project Must Include

1. Topic Explanation

Explain what your topic is and where it appears in real life.

2. Balanced Chemical Equation

Include at least one correct balanced equation connected to your topic.

3. Mole Ratio / Stoichiometry Connection

Explain how chemists use mole ratios to predict or control the amounts of substances involved.

4. One Sample Calculation

Show one stoichiometry calculation related to your topic. Work must be shown clearly.

5. Why It Matters

Explain why this chemistry matters in everyday life, medicine, engineering, the environment, industry, food, safety, or technology.

2-Day Timeline

Day 1: Research + Build

  • Choose your topic.
  • Find a balanced equation connected to it.
  • Research how the reaction works in real life.
  • Write down your mole ratio explanation.
  • Start your visual or presentation format.

Day 2: Finish + Present

  • Complete your sample stoichiometry calculation.
  • Make your explanation clear and polished.
  • Practice presenting your chemistry.
  • Present in the format you choose.

Present It However You Want

You will present your chemistry topic, but you may decide how to do it. Pick a format that lets you explain your ideas clearly.

Mini Slides

Create 3 to 5 slides and explain your chemistry to the class.

Poster or Infographic

Design a visual display and talk through it.

Recorded Video

Record yourself explaining the chemistry and submit the video.

Demonstration Style

Use props, drawings, or a model while you explain your topic.

One-Page Visual Report

Create a polished one-page document and present the main ideas.

Teacher-Approved Format

Have another idea? Get it approved and make it work.

Important: The format is your choice, but the chemistry still has to be accurate, clear, and complete.
