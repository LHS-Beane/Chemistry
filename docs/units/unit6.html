Why Atoms Bond

Atoms bond to become more stable by achieving a full valence shell.

  • Stability = lower potential energy
  • Valence electrons determine reactivity
  • Metals lose electrons, nonmetals gain or share

Ions & Charges

An ion is an atom with a charge due to gaining or losing electrons.

  • Cations: positive, formed by losing electrons
  • Anions: negative, formed by gaining electrons, end in -ide

Naming Compounds

Ionic compounds: metal + nonmetal

Balance charges to write formulas.

Roman numerals are used when metals have multiple charges.

Molecular compounds: two nonmetals, use prefixes.

  • No “mono-” on the first element
  • Second element ends in -ide

Acids

Acids are covalent compounds that produce H⁺ in water.

  • -ide → hydro– –ic acid
  • -ate → –ic acid
  • -ite → –ous acid
